Vanoise national park

Located in the heart of the French Alps, the Vanoise National Park stands as the first national park established in France.[2] This majestic mountainous region boasts soaring peaks, glacial valleys, and unique flock or herd of alpine biomes. The park offers an extensive network of hiking trails, providing visitors with the opportunity to explore its grand landscapes, to spot rare wildlife such as ibex and chamois, and to take a breath of the crisp mountain air. Besides, there are more than 100 bird species in the protected area, including bearded vulture, golden eagle and Eurasian eagle-owl, making this area a crucial place for species and ecological protection.

Pyrenees National Park

In southwestern France, the Pyrenees National Park stretches along the border with Spain, offering a diverse range of landforms. Towering snow-capped peaks, deep valleys, and cascading waterfalls characterize this rugged and amazing natural paradise. Hikers can traverse the famous GR10 trail, which winds through the park, offering breathtaking views and encounters with wildlife such as brown bears and marmots. The Pyrenees National Park is also a designated International Dark Sky Reserve, making it an ideal destination for stargazing.

Camargue Regional Nature Park

Nestled in the southeastern region of France, the Camargue Regional Nature Park is a captivating and diverse natural wonder. Stretching over 80,000 hectares, this enchanting park is known for its stunning landscapes and unique wildlife. With its expansive wetlands, salt marshes, and lagoons, the Camargue provides a haven for a wide array of species. Visitors can witness the amazing sight of wild white horses, pink flamingos gracefully wading through shallow waters, and flocks of migratory birds soaring through the skies. With its untouched beauty and rich natural heritage, the Camargue Natural Reserve offers a truly immersive experience for nature enthusiasts and wildlife lovers.

Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon is one of the most iconic natural wonders of the world, located in the southwestern United States. Carved by the Colorado River over millions of years, it stretches over 277 miles long and reaches depths of more than a mile. Its breathtaking landscapes and unique geological features make it a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts and adventurers.

The Grand Canyon offers visitors the opportunity to witness the incredible forces of erosion that have shaped the Earth's surface over millennia. Its multi-colored rock layers reveal the history of our planet, with each layer representing a different geological era. Hiking along the rim or venturing into the canyon itself provides a chance to explore this geological marvel up close.

Lake Baikal

Lake Baikal, situated in Siberia, Russia, is the world's deepest and oldest freshwater lake. It is renowned for its stunning natural beauty and unique ecological significance. This vast and crystal-clear lake is surrounded by picturesque landscapes, including rugged mountains, dense forests, and charming villages. Lake Baikal is also known for its incredible biodiversity, with numerous species found nowhere else on Earth.

President's Mountain (Presidential Range)

The Presidential Range, often referred to as President's Mountain, is a majestic mountain range located in the White Mountains of New Hampshire, USA. It is a favorite destination for outdoor enthusiasts, hikers, and nature lovers. The range is named after the United States Presidents and offers some of the most iconic hiking experiences in the region.

With its dramatic peaks, including Mount Washington, the highest peak in the Northeastern United States, the Presidential Range provides breathtaking vistas and challenging trails. Hikers can explore a variety of trails, from the Presidential Traverse, a grueling but rewarding hike, to more accessible trails suitable for all skill levels.
