Double Chocolate Cookies

Origin: Michigan Source: Family Recipe Category: Dessert

My daughter learned to make these cookies at a baking camp at Zingermanns and has tweaked the recipe to fit the taste buds of her siblings. They are extremely sugary so the salt helps to balance it. Note, these cookies are best eaten very quickly.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Packed light or dark brown sugar
  • Large egg
  • Pure vanilla extract
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chunks (melted)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Natural unsweetened cocoa powder
  • Baking soda
  • Salt
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chunks

Recipe Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar
  2. Add the egg and vanilla extract and beat well
  3. Add the melted chocolate
  4. In a separate bowl combine the flour, baking soda, cocoa powder and salt
  5. Combine the wet and dry ingredients
  6. Add the unmelted chocolate chunks.
  7. Form 15 cookies and place on a baking sheet.
  8. Cook for 12 to 13 minutes at 350 degrees.

Ratatouille

Origin: Provence, France Source: Traditional Recipe Category: Main Dish

Ratatouille is a classic French dish originating from the beautiful region of Provence. It's a delightful medley of fresh vegetables and herbs, stewed to perfection. This dish captures the essence of Southern France and is a favorite among vegetarians and food enthusiasts.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Eggplant
  • Zucchini
  • Red bell peppers
  • Onions
  • Garlic cloves
  • Tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Thyme
  • Rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe Steps

  1. Chop the eggplant, zucchini, red bell peppers, and onions into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Saute the garlic in olive oil in a large pan until fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped onions and cook until they become translucent.
  4. Add the eggplant and cook until it begins to soften.
  5. Stir in the zucchini, red bell peppers, and tomatoes.
  6. Season with thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper.
  7. Cover and simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  8. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

Italian Beef Stew

Origin: Italy Category: Main Course

This hearty Italian Beef Stew is a comforting dish that brings the flavors of Italy to your table. It's perfect for chilly evenings and family gatherings, and it's sure to be a crowd-pleaser.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 1.5 pounds of beef stew meat, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (for thickening)

Recipe Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the beef cubes and brown them on all sides. Remove them from the pot and set them aside.
  3. Add the chopped onion and garlic to the pot and saut√© until fragrant.
  4. Add the carrots and celery and cook for a few minutes.
  5. Return the beef to the pot and add the diced tomatoes, beef broth, red wine (if using), thyme, rosemary, salt, and black pepper. Stir to combine.
  6. Cover and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender.
  7. In a small bowl, mix the flour with a little water to create a paste. Stir it into the stew to thicken the sauce.
  8. Serve the Italian Beef Stew hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.
